Introduction

Periya Puranam

The Greatest Epic of Saivism

❀

The Periya Puranam (பெரிய புராணம்), also known as Thiruthondar Puranam, stands as the crowning jewel of Tamil Shaiva devotional literature. Composed by the poet-saint Sekkizhar at the behest of Chola king Kulothungga II in the 12th century CE, it narrates the sacred lives of the Sixty-Three Nayanmars — the great devotees of Lord Shiva who attained liberation through pure, uncompromising devotion.

The work is enshrined as the Twelfth Thirumurai, the final and crowning volume of the Shaiva Tamil canon. The Thirumurai comprises twelve volumes of hymns and devotional poetry; the first seven contain the Tevaram hymns of the three principal poet-saints: Thirugnana Sambandar, Appar, and Sundaramurthi Nayanar.

"The Periya Puranam does not merely describe devotion — it is devotion, crystallized into verse, a living flame that has illumined the hearts of Tamil Shaivites across a thousand years."

The sixty-three Nayanmars came from every stratum of society — kings, farmers, merchants, hunters, potters, and weavers. Their diversity is itself a teaching: the grace of Lord Shiva respects no boundary of birth, occupation, or learning. What alone matters is the sincerity and totality of surrender.

Sekkizhar composed approximately 4,286 verses organized into thirteen Sarukkams (divisions). The impetus for this monumental work was the king's desire for a poem that would surpass all secular literature — a poem whose subject was not earthly glory but divine devotion in its most radical, transformative forms.

Sundaramurthi Nayanar's earlier composition, the Thiruthondar Thogai, provided the sacred list of all sixty-three saints from which Sekkizhar drew his inspiration and narrative framework. Each saint's story is a unique window into the infinite possibilities of surrender to the Divine.

Structure of the Sacred Work

The Thirteen Sarukkams

Sacred Divisions of the Periya Puranam

❀

The Periya Puranam is organized into thirteen divisions called Sarukkams. Each takes its name from a verse or phrase within the Tamil text that captures the essential quality of the saints it contains. Together they span the full breadth of human experience illuminated by divine grace.
